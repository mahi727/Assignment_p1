About CS Department

The Computer Science Department at George Mason University (GMU) is a leader in computing education and research. Offering a dynamic and innovative learning environment, the department is at the forefront of solving some of the most complex challenges in technology today. With strong ties to industry, government, and research institutions in the Washington, D.C., area, the department provides students with unparalleled opportunities to engage in cutting-edge research and real-world applications.

Diverse Degree Programs

The department offers a range of degree programs to cater to the diverse interests and career goals of its students. These programs include a Bachelor of Science (BS), Master of Science (MS), and a Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Computer Science. The MS in Computer Science is a highly sought-after program that prepares students for professional and research careers in a wide range of areas, including artificial intelligence, software engineering, cybersecurity, and data science.
In addition to the core CS programs, the department also collaborates with other departments to offer interdisciplinary programs such as Data Analytics Engineering, Cyber Security Engineering and others.

World-Class Faculty and Research

The Computer Science Department is housed within GMU’s College of Engineering and Computing (CEC), which provides access to state-of-the-art labs and research facilities. The department also benefits from its proximity to the Washington, D.C., area, offering students access to high-tech industries, government agencies, and research labs.

State-of-the-Art Facilities and Resources

GMU's CS Department boasts a team of world-class faculty members who are renowned for their expertise and contributions to the computing field. These faculty members lead cutting-edge research in areas such as machine learning, robotics, cybersecurity, and bioinformatics. Students are encouraged to collaborate with faculty on research projects, providing hands-on experience that prepares them for careers in both industry and academia.

Strong Industry Connections

GMU's location in Northern Virginia provides students with numerous internship and job opportunities at leading technology companies, federal agencies, and research institutions. The department regularly hosts career fairs, networking events, and guest lectures from industry professionals, helping students establish important connections and build their professional networks.
